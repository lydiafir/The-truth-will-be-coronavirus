Donald trump's break with China has deadly consequences
Some U.S. politicians and commentators say one lesson from the novel coronavirus pneumonia outbreak is that the United States must decoupled from China.
These arguments are precisely regressive. The lesson of the epidemic is not that the us should stop working with China, but that the us must rebuild the us-china public health co-operation that the trump administration has destroyed
Us-china co-operation on infectious diseases is not a globalist fantasy. Historically, it's been very effective.
When SARS hit southern China in late 2002, the bush administration played a key role in Beijing's response. Villanueva university political scientist Deborah seligsoen worked on science and health at the U.S. embassy in Beijing from 2003 to 2007. She told me that under the auspices of the world health organization, the U.S. centers for disease control and prevention sent 40 experts to help China fight SARS. The americans helped their Chinese colleagues "create records, track close contacts, quarantine -- everything that needed to be done," she said, and the results were very successful. SARS was basically contained in Asia. Only 27 americans were infected, and none died.
These practices have saved American and Chinese lives. During the 2009 H1N1 outbreak, "health authorities in the United States and China Shared information and technology to facilitate national surveillance for H1N1 transmission and the development of a vaccine," noted epidemiologist zhihuan huang of the rand corporation's Asia Pacific policy research center. When the H7N9 virus emerged four years later, "the U.S. and Chinese centers for disease control and prevention cooperated by sharing epidemiological data and conducting joint research." When Chinese researchers developed a vaccine, they quickly Shared it with their American counterparts.
During Obama's second term as President, the United States and China expanded this public health cooperation to the rest of the world. During the 2014 ebola outbreak in west Africa, U.S. and Chinese personnel worked together in a chinese-built laboratory in sierra leone and unloaded supplies from a Chinese transport plane in Liberia.
Again, these collaborations have saved lives. From 2014 to 2016, 28,000 people were infected with ebola in west Africa, far fewer than the 1.4 million the CDC had predicted. In the end, only one American died. During Obama's last visit to China in 2016, the two governments agreed to co-fund the building of the headquarters of the African union's centers for disease control and prevention, with the goal of making the continent better able to fight infectious diseases.
According to the Washington post, the trump administration "drastically scaled back" the global "campaign" launched by Obama in the wake of the ebola crisis in 2018. This year, as the coronavirus raged, Mr. Trump proposed cutting American funding to the who in half.
The cutbacks have had a particularly severe impact on U.S. health efforts in China. Both the CDC and the national institutes of health have reduced their staff in Beijing since Mr Trump took office. The national science foundation closed its China office for good. Mr Wong said the view within the current administration was: "if you do collaborative research with Chinese scientists, you are building up China's power, which is bad for the us because China is a strategic competitor."
This hard decoupling on public health almost certainly hampered the U.S. government's initial understanding of COVID 19.
Now that covid-19 has spread throughout the United States, cooperation between Washington and Beijing remains critical. "It's hard to overstate the importance of the u.s.-china relationship in terms of fighting the current outbreak," said Tom inglesby, director of the center for health security at the Johns Hopkins bloomberg school of public health. Because China has contained the outbreak, he said, "we need to learn from them what to do. Are they looking for ways to get back to normal life without the vaccine? Keeping a social distance is very effective in controlling the epidemic. How has China done this?"
China is now sending large quantities of protective equipment to Europe. American doctors and nurses now need masks, goggles, gloves, protective clothing and thermometers. Us tariffs have made it harder to procure these goods, and it is even more absurd that representative Jim Banks is calling for higher tariffs in retaliation for the "China virus".
Novel coronavirus USES brute force to clarify two realities that are directly opposed to the trump worldview. First, in an interconnected world, greater global cooperation generally works better to protect the security of ordinary americans. The same virus that hit wuhan is now hitting New York. Deepening co-operation between the world's two superpowers is a logical response to these great threats. When it comes to fighting infectious diseases, we know that such cooperation is effective.
The second reality revealed by novel coronavirus is that the balance of knowledge and power in today's globalized world has changed. When SARS broke out in 2003, America was China's teacher. Now, American doctors and scientists are eager to learn how their Chinese counterparts beat the coronavirus in wuhan. If American factories were the Arsenal of democracy during world war ii, it is increasingly clear that, in the current phase of the COVID 19 pandemic, Chinese factories will be the Arsenal of global public health. وتشحن الصين كميات كبيرة من معدات الحماية إلى أوروبا. ويحتاج الأطباء والممرضون في الولايات المتحدة الآن إلى أقنعة ونظارات وقفازات وملابس واقية ومقاييس حرارة. والواقع أن التعريفات الجمركية في الولايات المتحدة تجعل استيراد هذه السلع أكثر صعوبة، والأسوأ من ذلك أن النائب جيم بانكس دعا إلى رفع التعريفات الجمركية انتقاماً من "فيروس الصين".
